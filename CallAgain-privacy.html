Privacy Policy

Call Again – Redial Assistant

Last updated: June 2, 2026

Our Commitment to Your Privacy

Call Again is built on a simple principle: your data stays on your device, always. We do not collect, transmit, or store any personal information on external servers.

What We Do NOT Collect

  • No contacts access — We never request or read your phone contacts.
  • No network connections — Call Again operates entirely offline. We make zero outbound network requests.
  • No phone numbers uploaded — Any numbers you enter or save in favorites remain exclusively on your device.
  • No usage analytics — We do not track how you use the app.
  • No advertising — Call Again contains no ads and no ad SDKs.

Local Data Storage

Call Again stores your favorites and settings locally on your device using Apple's SwiftData and UserDefaults frameworks. This data is:

  • Stored only on your device
  • Accessible only to Call Again
  • Automatically deleted when you uninstall the app
  • Backed up via iCloud if you have iCloud Backup enabled (controlled entirely by Apple and your device settings)

Notification Permission (Optional)

Call Again may request permission to send local notifications, which are used solely to remind you to retry a call at your chosen interval. These notifications:

  • Are scheduled entirely locally on your device
  • Are never sent to any server
  • Can be denied or revoked at any time in iOS Settings

All Calls Are User-Initiated

Call Again does not make automatic calls, does not operate in the background to place calls, and does not circumvent any iOS restrictions. Every call is triggered by a deliberate tap from you.

Children's Privacy

Call Again does not collect any information from users of any age and is safe for general audiences.

Changes to This Policy

If we update this privacy policy, we will post the updated version here. Given that we collect no data, material changes are unlikely.
