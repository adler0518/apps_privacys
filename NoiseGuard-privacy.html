Privacy Policy

NoiseGuard — Last updated: May 18, 2026

1. Introduction

NoiseGuard ("the App", "we", "us") is a sound level meter and noise logger designed for iPhone. This Privacy Policy explains how NoiseGuard handles information when you use the App. We have built NoiseGuard with privacy as a first-class principle: the App does not require an account, does not connect to our servers, and does not transmit your data anywhere.

2. Information We Do NOT Collect

NoiseGuard does not collect, transmit, sell, or share any of the following:

  • Personal identifiers (name, email, phone number, Apple ID)
  • Device identifiers (IDFA, advertising ID)
  • Usage analytics or crash reports sent to third parties
  • Audio recordings or decibel readings (these never leave your device)
  • Precise or coarse location (unless you actively save a record while on-device location is available)

3. Data Stored on Your Device

The following data is created and stored locally on your iPhone, accessible only by NoiseGuard:

  • Decibel samples and session statistics — stored in iOS UserDefaults for the History feature.
  • Audio recordings — saved as .m4a files inside the App's private Documents directory when you tap the record button. They are never uploaded.
  • App settings — your alert threshold, vibration preference, and similar UI options.
  • Optional location stamps — latitude/longitude attached to a saved record only if location permission is granted and you choose to save the session.

This data is fully under your control and is deleted when you delete the App.

5. Third-Party Services

NoiseGuard does not integrate any third-party analytics, advertising, crash reporting, or attribution SDKs. The App is fully offline-first and contains no network calls to external endpoints.

6. Data Security

Because NoiseGuard does not transmit your data, there is no server-side risk surface. On-device data is protected by standard iOS sandboxing and the device's hardware-backed encryption when your device is locked with a passcode, Face ID, or Touch ID.

7. Data Deletion

To delete all NoiseGuard data:

  • Open History and remove individual records, or
  • Delete the NoiseGuard app from your device. iOS will remove its sandbox, including all decibel records, audio files, and settings.

8. Children's Privacy

NoiseGuard is suitable for general audiences and does not knowingly collect any personal information from children. The App functions identically for all users and contains no in-app purchases, ads, or external links to user-generated content.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in the App or in legal requirements. Updates will be posted on this page with a revised "Last updated" date. Material changes will also be communicated through App Store release notes.
