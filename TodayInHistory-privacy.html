Privacy Policy

App: Today in History  ·  Last updated: May 19, 2026

1. Introduction

Welcome to Today in History ("we", "the app", "our"). We respect your privacy and are committed to building an app that is honest, simple, and safe. This Privacy Policy explains what data the app handles and how it is used. By using Today in History you agree to the practices described below.

2. Information We Do NOT Collect

Today in History does not collect, store, or transmit any personally identifiable information. Specifically, we do not collect:

  • Your name, email address, phone number, or any account credentials
  • Your precise or approximate location
  • Your contacts, photos, calendar events, or microphone/camera input
  • Device identifiers (IDFA, IDFV) for advertising or tracking purposes
  • Any usage analytics or behavioural fingerprints

3. Data Stored on Your Device

To deliver a smooth offline experience, the app caches the following locally on your device only. None of this data is transmitted to us or any third party:

  • Historical event data downloaded from the public Wikipedia "On This Day" API, cached for up to 24 hours and refreshed on demand.
  • App preferences such as language override, selected categories, and region filters, stored in standard iOS UserDefaults.
  • Bundled fallback dataset shipped inside the app for offline use.

You can clear all cached data at any time from the Settings screen, or simply delete the app.

4. Third-Party Services

The app makes anonymous, read-only HTTP requests to one external service:

  • Wikimedia REST API (api.wikimedia.org) — used to retrieve daily historical events. Requests contain only the date being queried and a User-Agent header. No personal information is included. Wikimedia's privacy policy applies to those requests: meta.wikimedia.org/wiki/Privacy_policy

The app does not embed any analytics, advertising, crash reporting, or social SDKs.

5. Data Security

All cached content is stored in the app's private sandbox provided by iOS. Network requests use HTTPS. Because no personal data is collected, there is nothing to leak should you lose your device — uninstalling the app permanently removes all locally stored content.

6. Data Deletion

To delete all data stored by the app, simply uninstall Today in History from your device. There is no server-side data to request deletion of, because we never receive any.

7. Children's Privacy

Today in History is suitable for all ages and does not knowingly collect any information from children. The app is rated for general audiences.

8. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page will always reflect the most recent revision. Continued use of the app after a change constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

9. Contact Us

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy, please contact us at: dev@example.com
