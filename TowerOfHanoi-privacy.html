Tower of Hanoi Privacy First

Privacy Policy — Effective May 22, 2026

1. Introduction

Tower of Hanoi ("the App") is a single-player logic puzzle game. This Privacy Policy explains how we handle information in connection with your use of the App. We are committed to protecting your privacy.

2. Information We Do NOT Collect

Tower of Hanoi does not collect, transmit, or share any personal information. Specifically, the App does not collect:

  • Name, email address, or any contact information
  • Device identifiers or advertising IDs
  • Location data
  • Usage analytics or crash reports sent to any server
  • Financial information or payment details
  • Photos, contacts, or any other sensitive data

3. Data Stored on Your Device

The App saves only one preference locally on your device using Apple's UserDefaults:

  • Difficulty setting (number of disks, range 3–8) — stored so your preferred difficulty is remembered between sessions.

This data never leaves your device and is automatically deleted when you uninstall the App.

4. Third-Party Services

Tower of Hanoi does not use any third-party SDKs, analytics services, advertising networks, or external APIs. There are no network requests of any kind.

5. Data Security

Because no personal data is collected or transmitted, there is no risk of your personal information being intercepted or misused through this App. The App operates entirely offline.

6. Data Deletion

All locally stored preferences are deleted automatically when you uninstall the App from your device. No account deletion request is needed because no account is ever created.

7. Children's Privacy

Tower of Hanoi does not collect any information from users of any age, including children under 13. The App is safe for all ages. We comply with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) and similar regulations.

8. Changes to This Policy

If this Privacy Policy is updated, the new version will be published at this same URL with an updated effective date. Continued use of the App after changes constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
