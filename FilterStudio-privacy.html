Privacy Policy

FilterStudio · Effective Date: May 18, 2026
100% On-Device · No Tracking

1. Introduction

FilterStudio ("we", "our", "the App") is a photo editing application for iPhone. We respect your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains, in plain language, what FilterStudio does and does not do with your data.

The short version: FilterStudio runs entirely on your device. We do not collect, transmit, sell, or share any personal information.

2. Information We Do NOT Collect

FilterStudio does not collect any of the following:

  • Personal identifiers (name, email, phone number, address)
  • Account credentials — the App has no account system
  • Device identifiers (IDFA, IDFV, advertising IDs)
  • Location data
  • Contacts, calendars, reminders, or health data
  • Browsing or usage analytics
  • Crash reports beyond Apple's standard, anonymous opt-in mechanism

3. Data Stored on Your Device

The following data is stored locally on your iPhone only, in the App's sandboxed storage. It never leaves your device.

  • Imported photos — when you select a photo from your Photos library, FilterStudio reads the image into memory for editing. The image is processed on-device using Apple Core Image and PencilKit.
  • Edit results — when you tap Save, the edited image is written back to your Photos library through Apple's PHPhotoLibrary API. We never upload or back up this image to any external server.
  • Preferences — your in-app settings (language preference, last-used filter) are stored in UserDefaults on this device.

4. Photos Library Access

FilterStudio requests two iOS permissions, both governed by the system permission dialog:

  • Photo Library — Read (NSPhotoLibraryUsageDescription): used only when you actively pick a photo to edit.
  • Photo Library — Add Only (NSPhotoLibraryAddUsageDescription): used only when you tap Save to write an edited image back to Photos.

You may decline either permission. Declining read access disables photo editing; declining add-only access disables saving. You can change these permissions anytime in iOS Settings → FilterStudio.

5. Third-Party Services

FilterStudio integrates no third-party SDKs. There are no ad networks, no analytics SDKs (Firebase / Google Analytics / Sensors / etc.), no social login, and no crash reporting beyond Apple's built-in system.

The only frameworks the App links against are Apple's own frameworks: SwiftUI, UIKit, PhotosUI, Core Image, and PencilKit.

6. Data Security

Because no data leaves your device, there is no transit channel to attack and no remote database to breach. Your data is protected by iOS sandbox isolation and your device's hardware-backed encryption (Secure Enclave). The App requires iOS 17.0 or later, which enforces modern app sandbox guarantees.

7. Data Deletion

To delete all data associated with FilterStudio, simply uninstall the App. This removes all in-app preferences and any temporary files. Photos saved to your iOS Photos library remain in your library and are managed by you.

8. Children's Privacy

FilterStudio does not knowingly collect any personal information from anyone, including children under 13. Because we collect nothing, there is nothing to associate with any age group. The App is rated 4+.

9. Changes to This Policy

If we materially change this Privacy Policy, we will update the "Effective Date" at the top of this page and update the link surfaced from the App's About / Settings screen. Continued use of the App after such changes constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
